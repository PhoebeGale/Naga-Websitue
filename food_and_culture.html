Festivities and Traditions

Naga City proudly celebrates the Dagitab Festival, also known as the "Festival of Lights," every December. This vibrant event features energetic street dancing, captivating cultural presentations, and a grand parade. It honors the city’s patron saint, St. Francis of Assisi, while symbolizing unity, hope, and progress.

In addition to the Dagitab Festival, barangays host annual fiestas dedicated to their patron saints, showcasing colorful parades, lively music, and communal feasts. Traditional dances and music often accompany these major events, reflecting Naga’s dynamic fusion of heritage and modernity.

Bites of Naga

Grilled Foods

Indulge in Naga's signature grilled delights, from juicy barbecue skewers to smoky liempo and freshly grilled squid, served with flavorful dipping sauces that elevate the experience.

Fresh Seafoods

Savor the ocean's bounty with Naga's fresh seafood offerings. From succulent crabs to perfectly grilled fish and tangy kinilaw, the city's coastal charm shines through every bite.

Street Foods

Experience the vibrant street food scene in Naga, Cebu, with must-try favorites like tuslob buwa, tempura, shakes, balut, siomai, ginabot, and more. These affordable and flavorful snacks capture the city's lively and communal spirit.
